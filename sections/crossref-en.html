Beam bridgec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/bridge#BeamBridge

The external characteristic of the beam bridge is usually the visible separation of the superstructure (bridge girder) from the substructure (supports, abutments) by bearings. The bearings transfer the loads from the superstructure to the substructures and give the bridge girder the necessary positional stability and movement capability. The cross-sectional shape in the longitudinal direction corresponds externally to a beam, usually the girder height is constant. Superstructures with variable superstructure heights are possible. As a rule, the bottom chord has a curvature corresponding to the moment load, it is vouched. The beam makes optimum use of the strength of the material and is used as a static system for conventional bridges with small to medium spans (approx. 80 m). Economical spans for beam bridges are up to 100 m, approx. 100-200 m for Extradosed bridges and from 200 m for cable-stayed bridges. The beam bridge is frequently used because of its comparatively simple construction. Many bridges over motorways are designed in this way. Beam bridges can be constructed in transverse direction with different cross-section geometries.

IRI: https://w3id.org/bridge#BoxGirderBridge

Bridge in which the main beams comprise girders in the shape of a hollow box. The box girder normally comprises either prestressed concrete, structural steel, or a composite of steel and reinforced concrete. The box is typically rectangular or trapezoidal in cross-section.
